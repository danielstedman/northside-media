# Northside Media — Complete Research Compendium

## The Company

**Northside Media Group** was a Brooklyn-based media and events company founded by brothers **Daniel Stedman** and **Scott Stedman**. Headquartered at 45 Main St. Suite 806, Brooklyn, NY 11201. Formally established as a parent company in 2012, though its roots trace back to 2003.

---

## The Founders

### Daniel Stedman
- American entrepreneur, filmmaker, producer, writer, and publisher from Maine
- Physics degree from Bates College
- Lived at Shakespeare and Company bookstore in Paris and at the Chelsea Hotel in NYC
- Won a **Teddy Award at the 2002 Berlin International Film Festival** for his short film "Celebration" — reportedly the youngest filmmaker ever invited to Berlin
- Later founded **Pressto** (2021), an educational writing platform
- Self-described "Cultural Engineer"

### Scott Stedman
- Co-founder and publisher
- Freelance writer
- Fought the famous boxing match at Gleason's Gym vs. NY Press
- Later moved to Boston to raise three children

### Other Key People
- **Jonny Diamond** — Founding editor of The L Magazine (2003-2013). Canadian-born. Went on to become Editor-in-Chief of Literary Hub
- **Mike Conklin** — Editor-in-Chief from 2013 of both The L Magazine and Brooklyn Magazine. Beer expert. Later became EIC of InsideHook
- **Nick Burry** — Publisher
- **Paddy Johnson** — Art Critic, nominated for Art Critic of the Year at 2010 Rob Pruitt Art Awards
- **Brian Quinn** — VP of Experience (Taste Talks)
- **Alexander Pipes** — Director of Marketing (Taste Talks)

## Brand Portfolio

### 1. The L Magazine (2003–2015)
- Free biweekly arts/culture publication
- Named after the L train connecting Brooklyn to Manhattan
- Inspired by Parisian event guides (Pariscope, l'Officiel des spectacles)
- Covered investigative journalism, arts, culture, event listings
- Distributed across Manhattan, Brooklyn, Queens, Hoboken
- Reached circulation of **106,600** by 2011
- Used bright orange distribution boxes (sparked rivalry with NY Press)
- Boxing match at Gleason's Gym later **fictionalized in HBO's "Bored to Death"** season finale — Zach Galifianakis portrayed Daniel Stedman
- Ceased print July 2015, resources shifted to Brooklyn Magazine

### 2. Brooklyn Magazine (2011–present)
- Launched March 2011 as glossy quarterly
- First cover star: Michael Pitt (Boardwalk Empire)
- Other early covers: Emily Mortimer, David Cross, Olivia Thirlby
- Tagline: "Dispatches from the better borough"
- BKMAG brand identity — bold geometric sans-serif logo
- Logo: https://www.bkmag.com/wp-content/themes/brooklyn-magazine/assets/images/bkmag-logo-black.svg
- "100 Most Influential People in Brooklyn Culture" annual list (covered by Columbia Journalism Review)
- Sold to Michael Bassik 2020, relaunched digital-first December 2020
- Acquired by BSE Global 2024
- Instagram: @brooklynmagazine

### 3. Northside Festival (2009–2018)
- Annual week-long June showcase of music, innovation, and art
- Called "the SXSW of the East Coast" by Reuters
- Grew from ~50 acts to **400+ bands across 50+ venues** in Williamsburg, Greenpoint, Bushwick
- Drew approximately **100,000 attendees**
- Music badges plus individual tickets available

**Notable performers across all years:**
Beirut, War on Drugs, Sharon Van Etten, Solange, Run the Jewels, Brian Wilson (Pet Sounds in full), Grandmaster Flash, GZA, Miguel, Dirty Projectors, Kamasi Washington, Courtney Barnett, CHVRCHES, Mac DeMarco, Vince Staples, Liz Phair, Soccer Mommy, Snail Mail, Black Flag, The Walkmen, Swans, Guided by Voices, Kacey Musgraves, Wolf Parade, Neko Case, Best Coast, Built to Spill, Thursday, Titus Andronicus, Liars, Fucked Up, Les Savy Fav, Deerhoof, Protomartyr, Kurt Vile, Wavves, Against Me!, Alvvays, Conor Oberst

**Key venues:**
McCarren Park (main outdoor, ~5,000 cap), 50 Kent, Baby's All Right, Brooklyn Bowl, Music Hall of Williamsburg, Rough Trade NYC, Knitting Factory, House of Vans, Warsaw, Wythe Hotel, Nitehawk Cinema, Glasslands, Death by Audio, 285 Kent, Shea Stadium, Silent Barn, Saint Vitus, Pete's Candy Store, Union Pool

**Innovation/speakers included:**
Mayor Bill de Blasio, Senator Kirsten Gillibrand, Glenn Greenwald, SNL's Sasheer Zamata, Ian Schrager, Piper Kerman (Orange Is the New Black), Kickstarter CEO Yancey Strickler

**Festival poster archive:** 150+ posters. Designer Gabi Lembo won GDUSA American Graphic Design Award 2016 for Northside poster. Brooklyn Magazine published retrospective calling them "cool, creative, crazy, wonky, and flat out weird."

### 4. SummerScreen (2005/2006–2018)
- Free outdoor film & live music series at McCarren Park, Williamsburg
- Six consecutive Wednesday nights in July/August
- Doors 6 PM, live music 6:30-7:30 PM, film at sundown (~8 PM)
- First screening: Spike Lee's "Do the Right Thing" at the drained McCarren Park Pool
- Over **4,000 people per screening**, ~24,000 per summer
- Rolling Stone called McCarren Park Pool "the coolest venue in the country"
- Music booked by legendary Brooklyn DIY promoter **Todd Patrick (Todd P)**
- Film curation: cult classics, 90s nostalgia — Back to the Future, Zoolander, Heathers, The Big Lebowski, Clueless, Jurassic Park, Mean Girls, Die Hard, When Harry Met Sally, Scream
- **2015 Todd P / Vitamin Water controversy:** Todd P pulled entire music lineup after Northside made sponsorship deal branding stage without consulting him or bands. "We would turn down the branding no matter what the money, but certainly if there is money changing hands, the bands should see it."
- Revived 2024 as "Paramount+ Movie Nights" under BSE Global/Brooklyn Magazine

## Cultural Significance

Northside Media was significant because it:

1. **Defined Brooklyn as a cultural brand** — among the first to treat Brooklyn as a distinct cultural identity worthy of its own media ecosystem
2. **Created "the SXSW of the East Coast"** — giving Brooklyn bands a hometown alternative to Austin
3. **Bridged culture and tech** — the NExT innovation component reflected Brooklyn's evolution from purely artistic to tech-creative hybrid
4. **Pioneered experiential media** — combining publications, festivals, food conferences, and outdoor screenings into an integrated media-and-events company before that model was widespread
5. **Documented a neighborhood's transformation** — the company's arc mirrored Williamsburg's gentrification from DIY indie scene to global cultural capital
6. **Launched careers** — many acts who played early Northside editions went on to major success (Sharon Van Etten, Courtney Barnett, Mac DeMarco, Solange, Soccer Mommy, Snail Mail, Alvvays, CHVRCHES, Vince Staples)
7. **Built community through discovery** — their tagline, and their core mission

---

## The Gentrification Story

The company's trajectory mirrored Williamsburg's transformation:
- Daniel Stedman: "Kent Avenue was basically one giant, long garbage strip" (before luxury condos)
- Iconic DIY venues closed: Glasslands, Death by Audio, 285 Kent — all within 18 months
- Festival shifted eastward into Bushwick as Williamsburg gentrified
- Corporate sponsors (MasterCard, Dell, Vitamin Water) replaced DIY ethos
- Both founders eventually left Williamsburg — Scott to Boston, Daniel to Prospect Heights

## The Collapse (2018-2020)

### Freelancer Payment Disputes (Gothamist, July 12, 2018, by Jake Offenhartz)
- Gothamist published "Boss Of Northside Fest & Brooklyn Mag Has Refused To Pay Workers For Years, Ex-Employees Say"
- **Important context:** Northside Media employees were always paid. The individuals cited in reporting (e.g., Sarah Zorn, Kat Lam) were freelancers/contractors, not employees. Zorn was never an employee of Northside Media.
- **The debts were owed by Zealot Networks**, which had acquired Northside Media in 2015 and accumulated obligations during its ownership period — not by Northside Media itself. When the Stedmans repurchased the company from Zealot in 2017, they inherited a financially damaged operation with debts Zealot had created.
- Media reporting often conflated Zealot's liabilities with Northside's, creating a misleading narrative about the company's management.

### The Grindr Grifter / Daniel Spence Scam (Observer, June 2019, by John H. Tucker)
In Northside Media's final days, the company was targeted by serial con artist **Daniel Spence**, a 32-year-old grifter who used dating apps to scam people across the U.S. Two of his targets were **Casey Holliday** (who worked for Northside) and **Daniel Stedman** himself.

- Spence approached Stedman offering to inject millions into Northside in exchange for becoming CEO, at a time when Stedman desperately sought investors to save the struggling company
- Spence fabricated a backstory: claimed to be the protégé of "Uncle Roy" and to have sold his family media company for $60 million
- Displayed a bank account showing **$150 million** on a phone app, and provided SEC documents as "proof" of wealth
- By mid-July, negotiations progressed. Spence's law firm indicated final paperwork would arrive within the week
- Spence's offer was "more than enough to reimburse everyone owed by the business and put us on a healthy pathway forward," Stedman said
- International law firm **Perkins Coie** represented Northside; a signing party was scheduled
- Days before the signing, Stedman was scheduled to meet Spence at a SummerScreen outdoor film screening — Spence didn't show
- **Spence was arrested for theft, embezzlement, and parole violations** — days away from "closing" the deal
- Spence had bilked victims of hundreds of thousands through Ponzi-like schemes, shell companies, and lies
- The arrest effectively marked the end of Northside Media

### Final Collapse
- By May 2019: company leadership disappeared, phone disconnected, Stedman removed from LLC filings
- No festival or SummerScreen in 2019 (failed to apply for parks permits)
- Brooklyn Magazine laid off most staff by February 2019; website went offline
- Brooklyn Magazine sold to Michael Bassik in 2020; all other properties ceased
- 2020: Brooklyn Magazine relaunched digital-first under Bassik/Brian Braiker
- 2024: Acquired by BSE Global (Brooklyn Nets owners Joe Tsai/Clara Wu Tsai)
