Taste Talks

Food and Culture. Not Just Foodie Culture. · 2013–2017

Taste Talks launched in Brooklyn and grew into a national food festival spanning four cities. Curated by some of the most influential figures in food — April Bloomfield, Danny Bowien, Questlove, Sean Brock, Kelis, Wylie Dufresne — and featuring everyone from Mario Batali to Action Bronson. Over five years it became one of the most important gatherings in American food culture, culminating in The Tastys awards.

2013

September 13–15 · Brooklyn · Inaugural year

Curator: April Bloomfield (The Spotted Pig). All-Star Cookout at East River State Park with 25 celebrity chefs. Cook It Raw Brooklyn brought international chefs for an impromptu outdoor meal using locally sourced ingredients.

2014

Brooklyn (Sept 12–14) + Chicago (Oct 3–5) · First expansion

Brooklyn Curator: Danny Bowien (Mission Chinese). Chicago Curator: Paul Kahan (Blackbird, Avec). Action Bronson debuted "Fuck, That's Delicious" — which became a major Viceland series. The free Future Food Expo launched. Action Bronson paired with Michael White to create Porchetta "Kra Prow" Tigelle at the All-Star BBQ.

2015

September 11–13 · Brooklyn

Curator: Questlove (The Roots / Tonight Show). George Takei appeared at the All-Star BBQ showcasing Fuji apple pie with miso caramel. Questlove DJ'd 80s-inspired sets. Kellogg's Cereal Throwdown won by Sam Mason. Rooftop Dessert Party with Dominique Ansel. Lea DeLaria (Orange Is the New Black) attended.

2016

Brooklyn + Chicago + Los Angeles (inaugural) · The Tastys launch

Brooklyn Curator: Sean Brock (Husk). Chicago Curator: Jason Hammel (Lula Cafe). LA Curator: Kelis. Glenn Kotche (Wilco) collaborated with Hammel on a multi-sensory dinner pairing food with music and visuals. The Tastys awards launched at BAM Howard Gilman Opera House, hosted by Mo Rocca.

Four cities: Brooklyn, Chicago, LA, Miami · Peak year
